My research pursuits revolve around the fascinating intersection of multi-modal learning in scenarios with limited data and interpretable machine learning, with a particular focus on learning from natural geometries present in the problems. I am passionate about creating automated systems for applications in healthcare, robotics, and social good, with a strong emphasis on geometric (e.g. graph) representation learning, reinforcement learning, and machine perception.

I am a first-year Data Science Ph.D. student at the University of Virginia, very fortunate to be advised by Professor Heman Shakeri.

Outside of research, I enjoy reading non-fiction books — particularly in (political) philosophy, ethics, and psychology. I am also an avid fan of football and chess, with Lionel Messi (G.O.A.T.!) and Magnus Carlsen among my all-time favorites.

Research

Md Fokhrul Islam, Sejuti Rahman  ·  May 2022 – July 2023
Relation and Knowledge Aware Zero-Shot Learning in 3D Object Recognition (Master's Thesis)

Proposed a novel multimodal embedding framework that aligns visual data from 2D images of 3D objects with graph-based semantic information via an attention-based fusion module, and a semi-supervised contrastive GAN module to overcome seen-class bias in generalized ZSL. Achieves a 3% improvement in harmonic mean on ModelNet40 over the strongest baseline (3DGenZ).

IHABOT: Intelligent Hospital Assistance Robot to Fight Contagion by Reducing Doctor-Patient Interaction

An autonomous hospital robot with autonomous navigation, physiological data collection, post-stroke rehabilitation exercise assessment, and COVID-19 diagnosis from radiography images using few-shot learning. Placed 2nd at the Dhaka University Research-Publication Fair, 2022.

An Intelligent Agent for Evaluating and Guiding Post-Stroke Rehabilitation Exercises (Undergraduate Thesis)

Adapted spatio-temporal GCN to predict continuous rehabilitation quality scores from variable-length RGBD videos, with self-attention over body joints to guide users toward better performance. Achieves state-of-the-art on KIMORE and UI-PRMD datasets. Work featured in two IEEE TNSRE papers and selected for a CVPR 2022 WiCV poster.

Mohammad Tareq, Md Fokhrul Islam, Swakshar Deb, Sejuti Rahman  ·  February 2022 – May 2022
Artificial Intelligence in Business Decision-Making: Code-Mixed and Transliterated Bangla Customer Reviews

Constructed an annotated Bangla-English code-mixed (BE-CM) dataset and proposed a data augmentation strategy for word embedding models (Word2Vec, FastText) to improve cross-lingual contextual understanding. XGBoost with FastText achieves 87% weighted F1 on the dataset. Collaboration with CARSHRM, University of Dhaka.
